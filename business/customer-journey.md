# Customer Journey Map for BuildFast

## 1. Aware
- **Trigger Event**: Indie hackers and creators encounter challenges in turning their software ideas into products due to lack of technical skills.
- **Friction Points**: Overwhelmed by the complexity of existing development tools; fear of failure; lack of awareness of no-code/low-code solutions.
- **User Emotions**: Frustration, confusion, hope for a simpler solution.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Provide educational content (blogs, webinars) that demystifies software development; showcase success stories of indie hackers using BuildFast.
- **Metric**: Number of website visits and engagement with educational content.

## 2. Consider
- **Trigger Event**: Users actively search for no-code/low-code platforms after learning about them.
- **Friction Points**: Difficulty comparing features and pricing; skepticism about the effectiveness of no-code solutions; concerns about scalability.
- **User Emotions**: Curiosity, cautious optimism, anxiety about making the right choice.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Offer free trials or demos; create comparison guides; provide testimonials and case studies highlighting successful builds.
- **Metric**: Number of trial sign-ups and demo requests.

## 3. Try
- **Trigger Event**: Users sign up for a trial or demo of BuildFast.
- **Friction Points**: Initial learning curve; potential technical issues during setup; limited support during trial period.
- **User Emotions**: Excitement mixed with apprehension; eagerness to see results.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Provide onboarding tutorials and responsive customer support; offer personalized assistance during the trial phase.
- **Metric**: Trial conversion rate and user engagement metrics during the trial.

## 4. Adopt
- **Trigger Event**: Users decide to subscribe to BuildFast after a successful trial.
- **Friction Points**: Concerns about ongoing costs; fear of commitment; uncertainty about long-term support and updates.
- **User Emotions**: Relief, satisfaction, but also lingering doubts.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Offer flexible pricing plans; ensure clear communication about support and updates; celebrate user milestones (e.g., first product launch).
- **Metric**: Subscription renewal rate and customer satisfaction scores.

## 5. Expand
- **Trigger Event**: Users successfully launch their first product and look for ways to enhance or scale their offerings.
- **Friction Points**: Limited knowledge of advanced features; potential integration issues with other tools.
- **User Emotions**: Pride in accomplishments, but also pressure to keep improving.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Provide advanced training resources; introduce a community forum for users to share experiences and tips; offer incentives for referrals or upgrades.
- **Metric**: User retention rate and upsell conversion rate.
