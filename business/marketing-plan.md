# marketing-plan.md

## Positioning
**Primary Positioning Statement:**  
Buildfast is a no-code/low-code platform designed for indie hackers and creators, enabling them to rapidly transform their software ideas into validated products without requiring extensive technical skills.

**Alternative Positioning Statements:**
1. Buildfast empowers creators to bring their software visions to life quickly and efficiently, democratizing product development for everyone.
2. With Buildfast, indie hackers can swiftly prototype and validate their ideas, reducing time-to-market and increasing their chances of success.
3. Buildfast simplifies the software development process, allowing non-technical users to create, test, and iterate on their projects with ease.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
1. **Persona 1: Indie Developer - Alex**
   - **Daily Job:** Works on side projects while maintaining a full-time job; seeks to validate software ideas quickly.
   - **Budget:** $50/month for tools and services.

2. **Persona 2: Entrepreneur - Jamie**
   - **Daily Job:** Runs a small startup and needs to prototype new features rapidly to keep up with market demands.
   - **Budget:** $200/month for development tools.

3. **Persona 3: Content Creator - Sam**
   - **Daily Job:** Creates digital products (e.g., courses, e-books) and wants to build accompanying software without hiring developers.
   - **Budget:** $100/month for software development tools.

## Channels
1. **Social Media Advertising (Facebook/Instagram)**
   - **CAC Estimate:** $30 per acquisition.
   
2. **Content Marketing (Blogs, Tutorials, Webinars)**
   - **CAC Estimate:** $20 per acquisition through organic traffic and lead magnets.
   
3. **Partnerships with Influencers in the Indie Hacker Community**
   - **CAC Estimate:** $50 per acquisition through affiliate marketing and co-promotions.

## Content Cadence
- **Week 1:** Launch blog post introducing Buildfast and its benefits.
- **Week 2:** Create a tutorial video on building a simple app with Buildfast.
- **Week 3:** Host a webinar featuring successful indie hackers using no-code tools.
- **Week 4:** Publish case studies showcasing early adopters and their success stories.
- **Week 5:** Release a blog post on the importance of rapid prototyping.
- **Week 6:** Share user-generated content and testimonials on social media.
- **Week 7:** Create a downloadable guide on best practices for no-code development.
- **Week 8:** Host a Q&A session on social media to engage potential users.
- **Week 9:** Publish a blog post comparing Buildfast with traditional development methods.
- **Week 10:** Release a video series on advanced features of Buildfast.
- **Week 11:** Share success stories from users who validated their ideas using Buildfast.
- **Week 12:** Conduct a survey to gather feedback and improve the platform.

## Launch Milestones
- **D-30:** Finalize product features and user interface; begin beta testing with select users.
- **D-0:** Official launch of Buildfast; initiate marketing campaigns across selected channels.
- **D+30:** Analyze user feedback; implement necessary adjustments and enhancements.
- **D+90:** Review performance metrics; prepare for a second wave of marketing efforts based on initial user data.

## Success Metrics
- **DAU (Daily Active Users):** Target of 1,000 DAUs by D+90.
- **$MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) Target:** $10,000 by D+90.
